How does the ranking system work?

The IQ Zoo uses an Elo rating system, similar to chess rankings. When you vote for one animal over another, their scores are adjusted based on the expected outcome. Higher-ranked animals are expected to win, so upsets create bigger score changes.

What do the rankings mean?

Animals are ranked from #1 (smartest) to #50 (least intelligent) based on their accumulated votes. The ranking "Ranked #4/50" means the animal is currently the 4th smartest out of 50 total animals.

How are the animals selected for comparison?

Animals are randomly paired for each matchup. This ensures all animals get fair opportunities to compete and their rankings reflect their true relative intelligence.

What do the score changes mean?

The numbers that appear (like +15 or -4) show how many points the animal's IQ score changed after your vote. Positive numbers mean they gained points, negative means they lost points.

Is my data saved?

Yes! All animal scores and rankings are automatically saved to your browser's localStorage. Your progress will be preserved even if you close and reopen the page.

How many animals are there?

There are 50 different animals in the IQ Zoo, ranging from highly intelligent creatures like dolphins and chimpanzees to simpler organisms like bacteria and amoebas.
